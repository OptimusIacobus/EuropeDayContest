The Euro Currency:

The Euro is the official currency of 19 EU countries: Austria, Belgium, Cyprus, Estonia, Finland, France, Germany, Greece, Ireland, Italy, Latvia, Lithuania, Luxembourg, Malta, the Netherlands, Portugal, Slovakia, Slovenia, and Spain. These countries are collected known as the euro area or eurozone, with currency regulation controlled by the Eurosystem, which consists of the European Central Bank in Frankfurt, Germany and the national banks of member nations. It was established as the official EU currency in 1992 through the Maastricht Treaty and remains as the official EU currency.
